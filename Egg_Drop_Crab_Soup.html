Egg Drop Crab Soup

Dive into a taste of the Vietnamese coast with our fragrant Egg Drop Crab Soup! Succulent crab meat simmers in a vibrant broth infused with aromatics, bursting with fresh seafood flavor. This isn't your average clam chowder - experience the unique balance of sweet, savory, and a touch of spice for an unforgettable meal.

Have all ingredients gathered (refer to concept: Sourcing Vietnamese Ingredients)
Ingredients:
    • 500 grams (about 1 pound) of cooked crab meat
    • 2 Eggs
    • 300 grams (about 10 ounces) mixed vegetables
    • 5 slices ginger
    • 1 bunch scallions
    • 100 grams (about 1 cup) tapioca starch or corn starch
    • 2.6 liters (about 11 cups) water
    • Chopped cilantro
  1. Prep the Aromatics and Crab: Mince the white part of the green onion. In a bowl, combine the crab meat, minced green onion, fish sauce, and a dash of pepper. Set aside.
  2. Prep the Egg and Tapioca Starch: Whisk the eggs in a separate bowl. In another bowl, combine the water and tapioca starch, stirring until the starch dissolves.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened about 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic, ginger, and minced green onion. Sauté for another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Simmer Broth: Add the chicken broth, fish sauce, chicken seasoning, mushroom seasoning, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add Vegetables: Stir in the mixed vegetables and simmer for another 3-4 minutes or until tender-crisp.
  6. Egg Drop: Reduce heat to low. While gently stirring the soup, slowly drizzle in the beaten eggs to create thin egg ribbons.
  7. Thicken the Soup: Pour the tapioca starch mixture into the soup while stirring continuously. Bring the soup to a gentle boil for a minute, allowing it to thicken slightly.
  8. Finish with Crab: Stir in the reserved crab meat and simmer for 2-3 minutes, just until heated through. Be careful not to overcook the crab, as it will become tough.
  9. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls. Garnish with chopped cilantro, sliced green onion tops, and a sprinkle of black pepper (optional).
Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently over low heat.
